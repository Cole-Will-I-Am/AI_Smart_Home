# Monetization & positioning strategy (plan of record)

Skeptical, no hype. Separates what is real from what must be built.

## Verdict
**Build — with a mandatory pivot and hard narrowing.** This is not an "AI smart-home app" or an
"estate OS." The defensible asset is the deterministic **permission-and-audit governor + local-first
resilience** pattern — *not* the AI, which is the biggest liability and least defensible feature.
Maturity: ~5% of a product (a strong reference implementation + a credible point of view, 62 offline
tests, zero real deployment). The viable business is a **managed catastrophe-prevention & oversight
service for multi-property owners**, kernelled on the engine, with the AI kept advisory. It is a
**services business with software leverage**, not a SaaS land-grab.

## Positioning
- **Category:** managed safety-and-oversight layer ("safety governor + flight recorder + cross-system
  coordinator") on top of existing estate automation.
- **Avoid:** DIY smart-home app, AV/scene control, voice assistant, "AI runs your home."
- **Wedge:** vacant-home **water + freeze + power resilience** with an auditable safety interlock and a
  single accountable operator. Concrete, insurance-relevant, fail-safe-toward-shutoff.
- **One-liner:** *"Catastrophe prevention for multi-property owners — auditable water, freeze, and
  power protection that keeps working when the internet doesn't, with a human always in control."*

## Customers & liability
- **Beachhead:** owner of 2–4 high-value properties (primary + seasonal/vacant) with an integrator, a
  monitored alarm, managed IT, and an estate manager / family office.
- **Pays:** owner / family office. **Installs:** certified integrator (existing channel). **Maintains:**
  us (managed service) + integrator. **Liability:** licensed pros own their scope; monitored life-safety
  stays independent & primary; our scope is software-advisory + monitoring under contract + E&O.

## Moat (honest: all ~0 today)
Real durable moat = **channel (certified integrators) + trust/operator-of-record + operational +
compliance/audit.** Technical/data moats are modest and erodable — do not overclaim.

## Revenue
Managed-monitoring **MRR + setup fees** are the business. Skeptical of energy-savings revenue
(attribution) and insurance add-ons (long cycle, actuarial proof) — 24-month plays, not launch lines.

## Trust rules (non-negotiable)
Never claim "prevents all X," "autonomous," "safe/certified," or "replaces your alarm." Life-safety is
**observe-only**, professionally installed, code-compliant, independent. Manual override always works.
**AI is recommend-only for high-impact systems in v1** — the deterministic engine executes.

## Roadmap
- **30d:** one wedge (water/freeze); durable state + hash-chained audit; auth; verified fail-safe on
  2–3 real device classes; minimal dashboard.
- **90d:** 1–3 homes; RBAC + operator dashboard; native life-safety independence; security review;
  monitoring + on-call; signed pilot + disclaimers + E&O.
- **6mo:** 3–10 properties, paying; incident response; avoided-loss proof.
- **12mo:** multi-tenant control plane; certified-integrator program; SLA tiers.
- **24mo:** integrator network at scale; insurer reporting pilot; data loop; operator-of-record brand.

## Kill criteria
Point solutions (Moen Flo/Phyn/Ting/alarm) are "good enough" and the platform premium evaporates; or
fail-safe can't be made reliable on real hardware; or no viable E&O/liability structure; or buyers
want convenience/AV not governance; or willingness-to-pay < cost-to-serve.

## Final recommendation
Build, but gate everything on two proofs: (1) verified fail-safe on real hardware, and (2) an estate
owner *paying* for a pilot. If either fails, pivot to a niche consulting/reference-architecture play
or keep the repo as a credibility artifact. Execution tracked in `docs/PRODUCTIZATION.md`.
